Active Looking

A Pedagogical Approach to Multimodal Compositions

About

This interactive essay is for my Computers and Composition course at Georgia State University, taught by Mary Hocks.

It is an attempt to come up with a new, useful pedagogical term specific to the field of Computers and Composition. In doing so, I have attempted to be as multimodal as possible while still maintaining the presence of content. I made the choice to hide the pages to create a more non-linear reading experience. As you will find out, you can go around and around in this essay, linking to the different pages from several different areas. I also decided to insert a few instances of links that go out into the world wide web, and I threw in a page that is completely non-sequitor. I made this choice because I want to remind the reader that papers hosted on the web, as this one is, do not exist in a vaccuum. And also because a little ridiculousness never hurt anyone while reading a paper.

That said, if you click on the picture of the sign at the top of each page, it should take you back to the introduction, if you should get lost.

The title picture is courtesy of http://www.flickr.com/photos/gozalewis/3329507901/ which I found through a creative commons search. All photos and music featured in this experience are creative commons items. I have credited them all, but if I forgot one, please let me know.

I hope you enjoy my essay.
